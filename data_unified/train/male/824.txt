So much his friend, ay, his unfeigned friend, That, if King Lewis vouchsafe to furnish us With some few bands of chosen soldiers, I'll undertake to land them on our coast And force the tyrant from his seat by war. 'Tis not his new-made bride shall succor him And as for Clarence, as my letters tell me, He's likely now to fall from him, For matching more for wanton lust than honour, Or than for strength and safety of our country.
