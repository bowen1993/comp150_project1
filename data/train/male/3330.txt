My good knave Eros, now thy captain is Even such a body: here I am Antony: Yet cannot hold this visible shape, my knave. I made these wars for Egypt: and the queen, Whose heart I thought I had, for she had mine; Which whilst it was mine had annex'd unto't A million more, now lost,she, Eros, has Pack'd cards with Csar, and false-play'd my glory Unto an enemy's triumph. Nay, weep not, gentle Eros; there is left us Ourselves to end ourselves. O, thy vile lady! She has robb'd me of my sword.
